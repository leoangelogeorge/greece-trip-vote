The Setup

  • After Ola & Awse's wedding in Athens (Aug 6-8), we have 6 nights (Aug 9-15) to explore Greece together.
  • The group: 4 couples + 2 kids (including a 3-year-old).
  • Starting & ending point: Athens (flying out Aug 15).
  • Goal: 2-3 days per island, with some Athens time built in.
  • Everyone is vegetarian-friendly.
  • We're open to splitting up for parts of the trip if people want different things.

Option A: "Cycladic Highlights" — Milos + Paros + Athens
Aug 9-10: Milos Aug 11-12: Paros Aug 13-14: Athens Aug 15: Fly out

The plan: 2 nights Milos → 2 nights Paros → 2 nights Athens.

Why: The best of the Cyclades in one trip. Milos has otherworldly landscapes, sea caves (Kleftiko boat tour!), and Sarakiniko's lunar beach. Paros brings charming Naoussa, incredible food, and beautiful beaches. Finish with 2 relaxed days exploring Athens (Acropolis, Plaka, rooftop dinners) before flying out.

Getting around: Ferry Athens→Milos (3.5h), Milos→Paros (~2.5h), Paros→Athens (3h). All by ferry, no flights needed.

Splitting up: From Paros, quick ferry to Naxos (40 min) or Santorini (1.5h) for anyone who wants a detour.

Estimated cost: €1,200-2,800 per couple for 6 nights

✓ Best Cycladic beaches, all-ferry route, relaxed Athens ending, great for kids on Paros

✗ Two ferry transit days, no Crete or Santorini in the base plan

Option B: "Island & Mainland" — Crete + Santorini + Athens Most Variety
Aug 9-11: Crete (Chania) Aug 12-13: Santorini Aug 14: Athens Aug 15: Fly out

The plan: 3 nights Crete → 2 nights Santorini → 1 night Athens.

Why: Crete is a world unto itself — the Venetian harbor of Chania, pink-sand Elafonisi beach, incredible Cretan cuisine (vegetarian paradise), and rich history. Then Santorini delivers the bucket-list caldera views, Oia sunsets, and wine tours. Quick flight back to Athens for a final evening together.

Getting around: Fly Athens→Chania (1h, ~€50pp), ferry Crete→Santorini (2h), fly Santorini→Athens (45min, ~€60pp).

Splitting up: Those skipping Santorini can stay longer in Crete or head back to Athens early for extra city time.

Estimated cost: €1,800-3,500 per couple for 6 nights

✓ Maximum variety (big island + caldera + city), Crete's food scene is incredible, the ultimate Greek highlights reel

✗ Most expensive option, Santorini is tricky with toddlers, involves 2 flights, only 1 night in Athens

Option C: "Off the Beaten Path" — Milos + Crete + Athens
Aug 9-10: Milos Aug 11-13: Crete (Chania) Aug 14: Athens Aug 15: Fly out

The plan: 2 nights Milos → 3 nights Crete → 1 night Athens.

Why: Milos for the dramatic coastline, sea caves, and turquoise water — equally photogenic as Santorini but far less crowded. Then Crete for 3 days of Venetian old town vibes, world-class Cretan cuisine (the best vegetarian food in Greece), and stunning beaches like Elafonisi and Balos.

Getting around: Ferry Athens→Milos (3.5h), ferry/fly Milos→Crete (ferry via Santorini ~4h, or seasonal direct flight), fly Crete→Athens (1h, ~€50pp).

Splitting up: From Milos, 1.5h ferry to Santorini for anyone who wants the caldera experience before continuing to Crete.

Estimated cost: €1,200-2,800 per couple for 6 nights

✓ Unique combo, avoids tourist crowds, amazing food on both islands, great value, Instagram-worthy

✗ Milos→Crete transit can be tricky (may need to connect via Santorini), less "classic postcard" feel

Option D: "The Grand Tour" — See it All
Aug 9-10: Milos Aug 11-12: Crete Aug 13: Santorini Aug 14: Naxos + Paros Aug 15: Athens → Fly out

The plan: 2 nights Milos → 2 nights Crete → 1 night Santorini → 1 night Paros (day trip Naxos) → morning ferry to Athens, fly out.

Why: The ultimate Greek sampler. Milos for lunar beaches and sea caves. Crete for Venetian Chania and epic food. Santorini for the bucket-list sunset. Naxos for a quick taste of the most family-friendly island. Paros for a farewell dinner in charming Naoussa. You see everything Greece has to offer in one trip.

Getting around: Ferry Athens→Milos (3.5h), fly Milos→Crete (~1h), ferry Crete→Santorini (2h), ferry Santorini→Naxos (2h), ferry Naxos→Paros (40min), ferry Paros→Athens (3h early morning).

Day trips: Naxos is a day visit from Santorini/Paros — see the Portara, explore the old town, grab lunch, then ferry to Paros for the evening. Paros could also be a day stop if you prefer flying Santorini→Athens directly.

Splitting up: Anyone wanting more beach time can skip Santorini and stay longer on Crete or Milos, rejoining the group on Paros.

Estimated cost: €1,500-3,200 per couple for 6 nights

✓ See 5 islands in one trip, maximum variety, incredible stories, every type of Greek experience

✗ Most hectic — moving every 1-2 days, 1 flight + 4 ferries, need afternoon flight on Aug 15, less time to relax per island

Important: This option requires booking an afternoon/evening flight out of Athens on Aug 15, since the morning ferry from Paros arrives ~10am. Alternatively, fly Santorini→Athens on Aug 14 and skip the Naxos/Paros leg.

Other Islands Worth Considering

These could be swapped into any option or added as day-trips. Suggest them in your vote!

Hydra

Car-free paradise with donkey transport
Just 1.5h from Athens by ferry. Gorgeous stone architecture, art galleries, and crystal-clear swimming spots. Perfect as a day-trip or overnight from Athens. No cars or motorbikes allowed — only donkeys!

Sifnos

The food capital of the Cyclades
Only 1h ferry from Milos. Famous for its cuisine (revithada chickpea stew!), traditional pottery workshops, and beautiful hiking trails between whitewashed villages. Less touristy than Paros.

Folegandros

Dramatic cliff-top Santorini alternative
Spectacular Chora village perched on cliffs with caldera-rivaling views. Peaceful, authentic, and crowd-free. Between Milos and Santorini by ferry (~1h each). The quiet romantic escape.

Naxos

Family-friendly beach paradise
40 min from Paros. Sandy beaches that stay ankle-deep for 50m out (amazing for toddlers). Affordable tavernas, mountain villages, and the iconic Portara temple gate. #1 rated family island.

Logistics to Know

  • August is peak season — book accommodation in the next 2-3 weeks.
  • We fly out of Athens Aug 15 — all options end back in Athens the night before.
  • Meltemi winds can delay high-speed ferries in Aug (conventional ferries are more reliable).
  • Ferry booking: Ferryhopper.com — book early for August!
  • Domestic flights (Athens↔Crete, Athens↔Santorini) are cheap (~€40-80pp) and take 45min-1h.
  • Villa/apartment rentals are better value than hotels for our group size.
  • Key ferry times: Paros↔Naxos (40min), Milos↔Sifnos (1h), Crete↔Santorini (2h), Athens↔Milos (3.5h).
